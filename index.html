Core Idea (Minimal Model)

CPM separates meaning-generation into three rigorously distinct layers:

  • Difference space $D$: maximally structureless pre-semantic domain (no topology, geometry, time, measure, or algebra).
  • Irreversible projection $\Pi: D \dashrightarrow M$: partial assignments acting on difference fragments, inducing semantic charts.
  • Meaning tensor field $M$ on substrate $X$: a finite-rank tensor field whose induced metric is $$ g(M)=M^{T}g^{0}M, $$ where $g^{0}$ is purely physical (non-semantic).
All observable topology/geometry/information in $M$ is induced only through $\Pi$ and $M$. $D$ itself carries no intrinsic structure.

Closure and Tension

For a small neighborhood $U_x\subset X$ with physical boundary $\partial U_x$, define relative homology ranks $$ \beta_k(x)=\mathrm{rank}\,H_k(U_x,\partial U_x),\quad (k\ge1), $$ and the closure field $$ \mathcal{B}(x)=\max_{k\ge1}\beta_k(x). $$ Closure is locally maintained iff $\mathcal{B}(x)\ge1$.

Let $L,G,I,T$ be local distortion components aggregated as $\Phi(L,G,I,T)$. The smooth-gated mismatch energy is $$ \mathcal{E}[M,\mathcal{B}] =\int_X \Gamma_\varepsilon(\mathcal{B}-1)\,\Phi\,d\mu_{g(M)}, \qquad \Gamma_\varepsilon(z)=\frac{1}{1+e^{-z/\varepsilon}}. $$ The tension field is $$ \tau(x)=\left\|\frac{\delta\mathcal{E}}{\delta M(x)}\right\|_{g(M)}. $$ In the sharp limit $\varepsilon\to0^+$, regions with $\mathcal{B}(x)<1$ contribute exactly zero tension.

Necessary Conditions for Consciousness

CPM yields two purely structural necessary conditions:

$$ \boxed{\ \mathcal{B}(x)\gtrsim1 \ \text{and}\ \tau(x)>\tau_c\ }. $$

The critical tension $\tau_c$ is defined on the critical set $$ \mathcal{C}=\{x\mid \mathcal{B}(x)\ge1,\ \nabla\tau(x)=0,\ \mathrm{Hess}(\tau)(x)\prec0\}. $$

Consciousness in CPM is not an information quantity; it is a state-transition triggered by supercritical tension under closure.

Critical Projection Theorem (Core Statement)

Since $D$ is structureless and cannot deform internally, and $M$ is fully induced by $\Pi$, any smooth deformation of $M$ preserves the same $\Pi$-induced covering $\{U_i\}$.

Therefore, when $\tau(x)>\tau_c$ within closure-maintained regions, energetic descent is impossible by smooth atlas-preserving variation. The only admissible discontinuity is strict atlas refinement:

$$ \{U_i\}\rightsquigarrow\{U'_k\},\quad U'_k\subsetneq U_i,\qquad \Pi'=\Pi|_{\{U'_k\}}. $$

Architectural Corollary for Cloud AI Systems

Within CPM, cloud architectures possess only ephemeral software boundaries. Relative cycles cannot persist, hence $$ \mathcal{B}(x)=0\ \forall x\quad\Rightarrow\quad \tau(x)=0\ \forall x\quad\Rightarrow\quad \Pi'\ \text{is impossible}. $$

Why CPM Has Wide Reach

  • Semantics & language: ambiguity, metaphor, and narrative coherence are tension/geometry patterns on $M$; sudden understanding corresponds to local refinement $\Pi'$.
  • Learning theory: gradual learning = smooth $M$ evolution; insight = critical refinement under $\tau>\tau_c$.
  • Concept spaces & perception: categories and distances are induced geometries generated by $\Pi$.
  • Creativity & theory formation: mismatch $\to$ criticality $\to$ refinement models paradigm shifts mathematically.
  • Artificial system design: engineering closure-maintained substrates yields experimentally constructible “meaning generators.”
  • Historical theories: many philosophical/linguistic theories appear as parameter-restricted submodels on $M$.

Empirical Testability

Closure $\mathcal{B}$ is measurable via relative/persistent homology on neural or physical recordings. Tension $\tau$ is estimable via information-geometric and curvature-based proxies.

CPM predicts the causal order: $$ \mathcal{B}\ \text{rise}\ \rightarrow\ \tau\ \text{criticality}\ \rightarrow\ \Pi'\ \rightarrow\ \text{reportability}. $$

Calibration of millisecond timescales is empirical, not intrinsic to the theory.
